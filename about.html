About Our Project

Project Description
Our project provides viewers with an insight on the progress of various countries with respect to renewable energy over time. Our format allows the viewer to get both a clear visual of worldwide energy change each year and a change over time breakdown of each individual country.
Vocabulary definitions
- input-equivalent primary energy: amount of fuel that would be required by thermal power stations to generate the reported electricity. It takes account of the inefficiencies in fossil fuel production and provides a better approximation of each source's share of "final energy" consumption.
- Primary energy in exajoules (EJ) has been converted to TWh by Our World in Data based on a conversion factor of 1,000,000 / 3,600 (~277.778).
- non-fossil based electricity sources: nuclear, hydro, wind, solar, geothermal, biomass in power, and other renewable sources.
- data calculated as gross generation and not accounting for cross-border electricity supply.

- Mappings: using the same color key to show the change in percent growth per year when you click on the country as the colors on macro-page.
- Constraints: can only click on countries on the world map that have micro-pages (unable to click on countries that do not have micro-pages).
- Affordances and Signifiers: on Becca's macro-page, it tells the user to click on countries, signifying that clicking will take them to the micro-page to see more information. otherwise, the user might not know that they can click, so we will include this on just one to show that the user is able to click, but no overcrowd smaller countries with this text.
